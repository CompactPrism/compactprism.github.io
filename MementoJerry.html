Memento Jerry (VR Project)

This project was my first time making a game for Virtual Reality. I chose to develop it inside Unity and ended up making what I call a “Disruptive Child Simulator” where you earn points by throwing objects around the room. I tried to make it as interactive as possible and as a result, there were a variety of objects to throw and mix together to make new objects.

Key Features

  • Slingshot: The slingshot included a slot where the player could put applicable objects inside and then shoot it at other characters for more range.
  • Watch UI: When making a VR game, user interface is a unique challenge you can’t just have a heads up display or have a panel appear in front of the player view. What I implemented was a watch similar to VR Chat or Rec Room where it has a button to open the menu and it could be interacted with similarly to a phone with a scrolling list of objects to throw.
  • Water: This was one of my more janky and inefficient features but also more interesting because of all of the ways that it was used. A water bottle could be bought which would drop water particles when held upside down. The cap would also stop the flow and each item could be thrown individually. The water would be able to act as a disrupter so the water could be flicked at other characters. Additionally, water could be poured into a glass which would slowly fill up. If the glass was thrown, it would smash on the surface and all of the water would spill out causing everyone to get disrupted. It was one of my favourite features I added next to the AI.
  • AI: This project was another step along my AI development journey where I took what I learned in the AI Programming module and applied it here to make an agent that could detect objects flying across the scene, search for the person who threw it and even included a system where if the player makes eye contact with the agent while they’re searching for the suspect, the agent will give the player a strike.
