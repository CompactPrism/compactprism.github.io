Junior Collaborative Project (Adrenaline Junkie)

This was my first time working on a team with dedicated roles and hierarchy. I worked on a team of about 25 members to create a parkour shooter in Unreal Engine 5. My role in this project got changed around towards the end but for the most part, I was the combat programmer. It is a role I hadn’t really worked on before this but I found it to be a great learning experience.

Key Features

  • Gun Class: Before actually making the mechanics, I started off with establishing a framework to build the guns off of. I made a class that was inherited by semi-automatic/automatic weapons and established a difference on whether they would shoot a projectile object or a raycast.
  • Critical Hits: Our game needed a way to make the player feel like they’re playing well so I was assigned to make a critical hit system where if certain hitboxes were hit by a projectile or a raycast, points would be added based on the combo system.
  • Ricochet: Towards the end of development our design team decided on more gun mechanics with one being the ricochet. This kind of shot was a reward for hitting a critical hit and would have the projectile/raycast target the next nearest enemy as long as it was in range. This was a massive challenge to implement with the different projectile types but once it was implemented, it worked really well however it didn’t really get shown off as the combo system was unbalanced.

Project Reflection

With this project, I learned a lot about communication between team members and suggesting ideas. While I mostly did what was asked until I ran out of tasks, our final product’s shortcomings encouraged me to become a better teammate for the collaborative project in the year after.
