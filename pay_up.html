Pay Up (Jan - Feb 2024)  

For my second semester in my first year, I worked on making a game inside Unity. By this point, I was already quite familiar with the Unity engine and so a lot of this was experimenting beyond the base requirements of the module. The game Pay Up is a top down 2D adventure game where the player just needs to collect money from completing quests in order to pay rent after being locked out of their house. It is a small open world and was built using tilemaps.  

Key Mechanics

Melee Combat

When attacking, the player just needs to click and they will start attacking in the direction they are clicking. This generates a hitbox that pushes back enemies and deals damage.

NPCs

I made a very basic NPC system with static characters placed around the map each with names and dialogue. They will face the player when spoken to and open up a dialogue box to display the dialogue. Dialogue was managed with a scriptable object which could be placed onto any NPC and different objects could be used for different purposes like delivering/receiving quests.

Quests

When a character has a quest, they will activate a separate dialogue sequence and then apply the quest object to the player's list of quests. The only quest type is a fetch quest where the player typically will deliver a message to another character and earn money from it similar to my text based game. I also made the menu which displays all the info for the quest.
