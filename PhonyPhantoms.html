Senior Collaborative Project (Phony Phantoms)

This was another reverse-phasmophobia game where the player plays as a ghost who scares the resident of a house. I worked with a team of 22 people. My role during this game was the AI programmer and I took what I learned while writing my dissertation to make a dynamic AI system with a Hierarchical Finite State Machine. I was able to make the AI have different reactions depending on how scared they were. I even implemented the framework of the scaring abilities so that the character would have different reactions based on whether they saw something, heard something, or are terrified.

Towards the end of the project, I noticed that there was a severe lack of leadership within the programming team so I had to step up and begin setting tasks for members and then bringing together everyone’s work to make it cohesive and functional.

Key Features

  • AI: As described above.
  • Abilities/Traps: Objects placed in the world that would be detected by the AI causing a reaction based on how scary it is.
  • Tutorial Level: We needed something for onboarding so I made a tutorial level which showcased all the basic mechanics for scaring besides the shop.
  • Day and Night Cycle: A pretty simple system which was integrated with the AI’s scheduling system to create a difference between the setup phase (Day) and the scaring phase (Night).
